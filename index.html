Whoami

Hello, I’m Krishna, a graduate student at The University of Texas at Dallas specializing in cybersecurity. My interest in security began during my undergrad, where I focused on the foundations of secure systems and network defense. That initial curiosity grew into a deeper passion as I worked on hands-on labs and real-world challenges.

I’ve completed more than 130 labs on TryHackMe, placing in the top 2% on the platform. These experiences have helped me understand real-world attacks, detection techniques, and how to think like both an attacker and a defender.

I hold the CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) certification and recently earned the Practical SOC Analyst Associate (PSAA) from TCM Security, where I focused on SOC core skills such as log analysis, threat hunting, and incident response.

I’ve also been involved in research, exploring how machine learning can improve intrusion detection. One project was published with IEEE, and another, which focused on explainable AI, was published in the Journal of Technology. I enjoy working at the intersection of practical security and research, and I’m always looking for ways to grow technically and strategically.

I’m looking to grow into a SOC Analyst role where I can apply these skills to help teams detect threats faster, respond smarter, and continuously improve their defenses.
