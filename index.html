Whoami

Hello, I'm Krishna, a graduate student at The University of Texas at Dallas specializing in cybersecurity (MS, expected May 2026). My passion for offensive and defensive security has driven me from foundational coursework to real-world exploitation — I've ranked Top 1% globally on TryHackMe (rank 9,877), completing 250+ hands-on labs across Red Team operations, Active Directory exploitation, and privilege escalation while maintaining a 440-day learning streak.

I hold the Practical Junior Penetration Tester (PJPT) and Practical IoT Pentest Associate (PIPA) certifications from TCM Security, in addition to CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) and PSAA. On the PJPT exam I achieved full domain compromise of an Active Directory forest via LLMNR poisoning, Kerberoasting, Pass-the-Hash, and Mimikatz.

In 2025, my team placed 1st at Toyota HackFesta, a two-day CTF focused on automotive cybersecurity, demonstrating CAN and Ethernet network defense. I'm also actively studying for the Practical Network Penetration Tester (PNPT) certification.

My research spans machine learning for intrusion detection and Mobile Edge Computing — published in IEEE ASSIC 2024 and the Journal of Technology. I've published 40+ CTF walkthroughs documenting adversarial methodologies and exploit chains.

I'm seeking opportunities in penetration testing, red teaming, or security operations where I can apply offensive skills to help organizations understand and reduce their attack surface.
